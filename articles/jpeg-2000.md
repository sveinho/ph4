JPEG 2000 allows images to be stored with exact mathematical pixel retention while achieving better compression ratios than traditional TIFF or JPEG formats.

#### 1. Recommendation Criteria
Recommended by several national bodies for archiving massive high-resolution digitized collections, such as historical maps, newspapers, and aerial satellite photography.

#### 2. Migration Risks
Software tool support is highly specialized. Migrating to or from JPEG 2000 using non-compliant open-source decoders can lead to subtle color space shifts or header metadata corruption if profiles are mismanaged.

#### 3. Technical Limitations
Requires significant computing power to encode and decode compared to standard formats. It is actively debated in the archival community due to lower adoption rates in commercial software, leading to long-term sustainability concerns.

### Format Identification
- **Byte Signature (Magic Bytes):** Starts with a signature box matching Hex `00 00 00 0C 6A 50 20 20 0D 0A 87 0A` (ASCII characters `jP  `).
- **Structural Analysis:** Evaluated by parsing the mandatory JPEG 2000 signature box, file type box, and header box structures.
- **Content Analysis:** Processed via JHOVE or VeraPDF, registered under PRONOM PUID `fmt/151` (for JP2 core format).

### Authority Information
- **Governing Body:** ISO/IEC 15444-1 (International Organization for Standardization).
- **Institutional Approval:** Formally adopted as an alternative master standard by the Library of Congress (LOC) and the Wellcome Library, though evaluated with caution by the National Archives of Norway.
